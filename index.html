Giant Pandas

Giant panda populations are found in south-central China; primarily in Gansu, Shaanxi, and Sichuan province. Not only have human activities such as agriculture and infrastructure developments changed the climate, but it also forced Giant Pandas to migrate to higher elevations where bamboo, a plant that makes up 90% of a panda’s diet, are less likely to grow. Giant pandas’ long generation time, low productivity rate, and lack of food source would limit the specie’s ability to adapt to climate change.

Coral Bleaching

Corals rely on their symbiotic relationship with a type of algae called zooxanthellae to survive. The algae is a food source and also gives the coral its vibrant color. As a result of rising sea temperature, the algae would leave the coral, leaving the coral colorless, fragile, and prone to diseases.

National Ocean Service

Polar Bears

Polar bears rely heavily on the sea ice environment in the Arctic region for traveling, hunting, mating, and resting. However, substantial global warming in the Arctic over the last 50 years has caused the polar bear to lose their living habitat. Arctic sea ice reaches its minimum extent in September of each year. The ice area has declined 13.4% per decade from 1979 through 2015; meaning that the ice could completely disappear during the month of September by mid-century.

Amazon Rainforest

Deforestation and flooding in the Amazon Rainforest has altered the regional climate and displaced thousands of animals. Along with human activities and rising sea levels, wildfire is also destroying the once-beautiful land. In 2019, Brazil's National Institute for Space Research (INPE) recorded more than 70,000 wildfires between Jan. 1 and Aug. 20, 2019. That is an 85% increase compared with the same period in 2018.

National Geographic & Mongabay

Madagascar

Madagascar is home to diverse and unique biodiversity, but it is also one of Africa's poorest countries. The human population in Madagascar relies largely on farming and fishing. However, Madagascar is commonly experiencing powerful cyclones that damage ecosystems and infrastructure, and climate change has increased the number and severity of extreme weather conditions. The extreme weather pattern destroys Madagascar's unique biodiversity and puts a burden on Madagascar's human population.
